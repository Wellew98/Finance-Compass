VAT Tips in SA

Understanding VAT in South Africa:

  • The standard VAT rate is currently 15%.
  • Some essential goods and services are Zero-Rated (VAT at 0%) or Exempt (no VAT charged).
  • Businesses registered for VAT collect VAT on sales (Output Tax) and can claim VAT back on expenses (Input Tax).
  • This calculator uses the standard 15% rate for estimations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about VAT in South Africa

What is VAT?

VAT stands for Value Added Tax. It is a consumption tax levied on the supply of goods and services in South Africa. Businesses registered for VAT act as agents for SARS, collecting VAT on behalf of the government.

What is the current standard VAT rate in South Africa?

The current standard VAT rate in South Africa is 15%. This rate has been in effect since April 1, 2018.

What are Zero-Rated and Exempt supplies?

Zero-Rated supplies are taxable supplies on which VAT is charged at a rate of 0%. Examples include most basic food items, paraffin, and international transport. Businesses making zero-rated supplies can still claim input tax on related expenses. Exempt supplies are supplies on which no VAT is charged. Examples include most financial services, educational services, and residential rentals. Businesses making only exempt supplies cannot claim input tax on related expenses.

How do I calculate VAT?

To calculate VAT at 15%:

  • If the amount EXCLUDES VAT: Multiply the amount by 0.15 to get the VAT amount. Add the VAT amount to the original amount to get the total including VAT. (Amount Excl. VAT * 1.15 = Amount Incl. VAT)
  • If the amount INCLUDES VAT: Divide the amount by 1.15 to get the amount excluding VAT. Subtract the amount excluding VAT from the total to get the VAT amount. (Amount Incl. VAT / 1.15 = Amount Excl. VAT)
