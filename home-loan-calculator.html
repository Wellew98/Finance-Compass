Understanding Home Loans in South Africa

A home loan (also called a mortgage or bond) is a long-term financial commitment. The bank provides you with funds to purchase property, and you repay this amount with interest over an agreed period.

Key Terms to Understand
  • Principal: The amount you borrow from the bank.
  • Interest Rate: The percentage the bank charges you for lending the money. In South Africa, rates are typically quoted as "Prime plus/minus X%".
  • Loan Term: The period over which you'll repay the loan, usually 20-30 years in South Africa.
  • Deposit: The initial payment you make towards the property. A larger deposit typically results in better interest rates.
  • Repayment: The monthly amount you pay, consisting of both principal and interest.
South African Transfer Costs

When buying property in South Africa, you'll need to budget for several additional costs beyond the purchase price:

  • Transfer Duty: A tax paid to SARS for property transfers. First-time buyers enjoy exemptions up to R1 million.
  • Bond Registration: Fee paid to the attorney who registers your bond.
  • Transfer Attorney Fees: Cost of transferring property ownership.
  • Deeds Office Fees: Government fee for registering the property transfer.
  • Initiation Fee: Bank fee for processing your home loan application.
  • Municipal Rates and Clearance Certificate: Covering the seller's municipal rates and taxes.

These costs can add between 8-10% to your purchase price, so budget accordingly.

Tips to Save on Your Home Loan

With strategic planning, you can save thousands on your bond:

  • Make Extra Payments: Even small additional monthly payments can substantially reduce your loan term and interest.
  • Save for a Larger Deposit: A 10-20% deposit can secure better interest rates and reduce your loan amount.
  • Negotiate Your Interest Rate: Shop around for the best rates and negotiate with your bank.
  • Consider Shorter Loan Terms: While monthly payments are higher, you'll pay less interest over time.
  • Review Your Home Loan Annually: Check if you can refinance at better rates or increase your payments.
  • Make Lump Sum Payments: Use bonuses or tax refunds to make lump sum payments to reduce your principal.

What is a good interest rate for a home loan in South Africa?

As of April 2025, the Prime lending rate in South Africa is around 10.75%. A good interest rate would typically be Prime + 0% to Prime - 0.5% for buyers with excellent credit profiles. First-time buyers might expect rates around Prime + 0.5% to Prime + 1%. Your actual rate depends on your credit score, income stability, deposit amount, and property value.

How much deposit do I need for a home loan in South Africa?

While 100% bonds (no deposit) are available for qualifying buyers, most banks prefer a minimum 10% deposit. A larger deposit (10-20%) often secures better interest rates and improves your application's chances. First-time homebuyers with good credit profiles may qualify for higher loan-to-value ratios with smaller deposits.

What additional costs should I budget for when buying property?

Beyond the purchase price, budget for:

  • Transfer duty (progressive tax starting at 0% for properties under R1m)

How can I improve my chances of home loan approval?

To improve your chances:
